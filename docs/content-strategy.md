## Content Gap Questions

### Comparative / Evaluative

- Which is better, the original Douay-Rheims or the Challoner revision?

- Is a translation from the Latin Vulgate more faithful than a translation from Hebrew and Greek?
- Which Catholic Bible is most accurate?

- Douay-Rheims vs NAB — which should Catholics use?

- Douay-Rheims vs Douay-Rheims Challoner — what changed?

### Theological / Verse-Level

- **Isaiah 7:14** — Does the Hebrew say "virgin" or "young woman"? Why does the Douay-Rheims (following the Vulgate and Septuagint) say "virgin" when modern Protestant translations say "young woman"? Why does this matter for Matthew 1:23?
- **Genesis 3:15** — Why does the Douay-Rheims say "she shall crush thy head" when other translations say "he"? The Latin *ipsa* vs *ipse* dispute and its Marian significance.
- **Matthew 6:11** — Why "supersubstantial bread" not "daily bread"? The Eucharistic interpretation of *supersubstantialem*.
- **Matthew 4:17** — Why "do penance" not "repent"? What *poenitentiam agite* actually means and why it matters for the sacrament of confession.
- **Matthew 1:25** — Why "till she brought forth" and what it does (and doesn't) say about Mary's perpetual virginity.
- **Luke 1:28** — Why "full of grace" (*gratia plena / kecharitomene*) and not "highly favored"? The translation and the Immaculate Conception.
- **1 Corinthians 13** — Why "charity" not "love"? What the Vulgate's *caritas* preserves that "love" loses.
- **1 John 5:7–8** — The Johannine Comma: why does the Douay-Rheims include the Trinitarian clause that most modern Bibles omit?
- **Romans 3:28** — Did Luther add the word "alone" to this verse? What the Douay-Rheims actually says.
- **James 2:24** — "faith and works" and the Protestant Reformation's central dispute.
- **Hebrews 11:21** — Why does the Douay-Rheims say Jacob "worshipping the top of his rod"? The staff/sceptre question.
- **2 Maccabees 12:46** — "It is a holy and wholesome thought to pray for the dead" — and why this verse is in the Catholic Bible but not the Protestant.
- **Acts 1:20** — "his bishopric let another take" (*episcopatum*) and what it says about apostolic succession.

## Why Isaiah 7:14 Is Priority #2

This is one of the most-searched Catholic/Protestant apologetics questions. The Douay-Rheims is uniquely positioned to answer it because:

- The Hebrew *almah* vs *bethulah* debate has a direct answer: Matthew 1:23 quotes the Septuagint, which uses *parthenos* (virgin), and Jerome's Vulgate followed the LXX — so the Douay-Rheims's "virgin" reflects the *received text of the New Testament's own quotation*, not a Catholic invention.
- No other Bible site explains this from the Vulgate's perspective.
- It drives traffic from apologetics searches, Protestant curiosity, and Catholic catechesis.

Genesis 3:15 (*ipsa* vs *ipse*) is the same category — a verse where the Douay-Rheims reading has a specific theological argument behind it that can be explained in depth.
