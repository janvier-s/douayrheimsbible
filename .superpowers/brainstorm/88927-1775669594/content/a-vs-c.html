A vs C — the key difference

One question: do you ever want to see the Bible text and the study notes at the same time on mobile?

A

Bottom Sheet — text + notes together

The text stays visible above while notes peek from the bottom. Drag up to read more notes, drag down to focus on text. Tapping a verse scrolls notes to that verse.

Best if: you want to read a verse, glance at the commentary, then keep reading — without leaving the page.

C

Tab Switcher — full screen each

Text and study panel each fill the whole screen. A tab bar at the top switches between them. Scroll position is preserved independently.

Best if: you read a passage, then switch to study mode to dig in — treating them as two separate reading sessions.

Implementation note

C is significantly simpler to build and has no risk of layout bugs. A is the richer UX but requires a drag-handle gesture system and careful scroll coordination between the sheet and the reading area.
