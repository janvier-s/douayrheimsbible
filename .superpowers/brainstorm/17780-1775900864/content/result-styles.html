Text Search Result Styles

Imagine the user searched for "thou art Peter" — how should results appear?

A

Full Verse + Highlight

Shows the complete verse text with matching words bolded. Grouped by chapter, just like your current verse search results.

Matthew 16:18

And I say to thee: That thou art Peter; and upon this rock I will build my church, and the gates of hell shall not prevail against it.

Read full chapter →

Pros

  • Full context — no guessing what the verse says
  • Consistent with your existing verse search results
  • Highlights make matches scannable
  • User can read and compare verses without clicking through

Cons

  • Takes more vertical space — fewer results visible at once
  • Common words like "the" could return hundreds of full verses
  • Slower to scan when you have many results
B

Snippet / Excerpt

Shows ~15 words around each match, like Google results. Trimmed with ellipsis.

Matthew 16:18

...I say to thee: That thou art Peter; and upon this rock I will build...

Read full chapter →

Pros

  • Compact — more results visible per screen
  • Faster to scan through many results
  • Focuses attention on the matching phrase

Cons

  • Loses context — can't fully understand the verse
  • Feels more "search engine" than "Bible reader"
  • Inconsistent with how verse search shows full verses
  • Users will click through more often to read the full verse
C

Compact Reference List

Dense list of references with a tiny preview. Maximum density.

Matt 16:18 ...That thou art Peter; and upon this rock...
John 21:17 ...thou knowest that I love thee...
Acts 10:14 ...for I have never eaten any thing that is common...

Pros

  • Maximum results per screen — great for "where is this phrase?"
  • Fast scanning of references
  • Lightest on rendering

Cons

  • Almost no context — you must click to read
  • Completely different feel from the rest of the site
  • Poor for studying or comparing verses
  • Feels like a concordance index, not a Bible reader
